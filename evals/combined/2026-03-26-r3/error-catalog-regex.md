## Error: Em-dash "to like X to" orphan fragments

**Goal:** Artifact Cleanliness (Goal 2)
**Severity:** medium
**Frequency:** 2/2 papers evaluated (2312.03893, confirmed; probably in any paper using `---text---` LaTeX)
**Paper(s):** 2312.03893

### What the scripter produced
> "And while some strive for alignment with a subset of humanity to like democratic governments to they still routinely struggle to realize the will of their constituencies."

> "This document is written for those who want the impact of these powerful systems to from governments to AGI to to align with the will of humanity."

> "the full will of humanity is perhaps an appropriate alignment target for systems to like the United Nations"

### What the correct output should be
> "And while some strive for alignment with a subset of humanity — like democratic governments — they still routinely struggle to realize the will of their constituencies."

> "This document is written for those who want the impact of these powerful systems — from governments to AGI — to align with the will of humanity."

### Source material
LaTeX likely uses paired `---` em-dashes or `\textemdash{}` + `\!` constructs:
```latex
strive for alignment with a subset of humanity---like democratic governments---they still...
```
The regex pipeline strips em-dash separators inconsistently, leaving "to" prepositions before and after the stripped inline example.

### Root cause (if identifiable)
`latex_parser.py` converts em-dashes to spaces or strips them without handling the surrounding prepositions. When the LaTeX uses `---like X---` as a parenthetical, both dashes are stripped leaving "to X to" double-preposition artifacts.

## Error: Inline figure reference floating punctuation (" . " artifacts)

**Goal:** Artifact Cleanliness (Goal 2)
**Severity:** medium
**Frequency:** 2/2 papers (2311.02242 has 6+ instances; likely in any paper with inline `\ref{}` figure calls)
**Paper(s):** 2311.02242

### What the scripter produced
> "...that kicks off a collective response process . During a collective response process, participants first share..."

> "...a representative subset of responses from the group . The moderator sees preliminary results..."

> "...and pair choice votes . However, when there are hundreds or thousands of participants..."

> "The final policies generated by the process for each issue can be found . We measured overall support..."

### What the correct output should be
> "...that kicks off a collective response process. During a collective response process, participants..."

> "...a representative subset of responses from the group. The moderator sees preliminary results..."

### Source material
```latex
...kicks off a collective response process (figure~\ref{fig:process}). During...
...a representative subset of responses from the group~\ref{fig:results}. The moderator...
```

### Root cause (if identifiable)
The 628cec3 commit cleaned up `(figure )` and `(table )` parenthetical forms but did not handle inline `~\ref{}` or bare `\ref{}` that appear after nouns without parentheses. After stripping, these leave a space before the sentence-terminal period. Fix: add a cleanup pass for ` \.` → `.` and ` \,` → `,` after reference stripping.

## Error: Inline "Author contributions:" metadata not stripped

**Goal:** Header/Footer Compliance (Goal 3)
**Severity:** medium
**Frequency:** 1/2 papers (2311.02242)
**Paper(s):** 2311.02242

### What the scripter produced
> "Author contributions: Andrew developed the AI tools used in the process. Andrew, Colin, and Lisa designed and tested the process. Aviv advised on process design and implementation. Everyone contributed to this report."

### What the correct output should be
This metadata section should be stripped entirely. It is not part of the scientific content.

### Source material
```latex
\textbf{Author contributions:} Andrew developed the AI tools...
```
or similar inline bold marker (not a `\section{}` heading).

### Root cause (if identifiable)
The 628cec3 commit added stripping of standalone "Author Contributions" section blocks (looking for `\section{Author Contributions}` or similar). However, this paper uses an inline `\textbf{Author contributions:}` marker within the body, which the section-level stripping regex does not match. Fix: add pattern matching for `Author contributions?:\s*[^\\n]+\n` inline markers.

## Error: Dangling "Code and data available at: " with no URL

**Goal:** Artifact Cleanliness (Goal 2)
**Severity:** low
**Frequency:** 1/2 papers (2311.02242)
**Paper(s):** 2311.02242

### What the scripter produced
> "Code and data available at: "

A complete sentence with no content following the colon.

### What the correct output should be
This line should be stripped entirely, or include the URL.

### Source material
```latex
Code and data available at: \url{https://github.com/...}
```
or similar. The URL was stripped but the surrounding text was not.

### Root cause (if identifiable)
`latex_parser.py` strips `\url{}` contents but does not clean up the surrounding "available at:" text when the URL is the only content after the colon. Fix: add pattern `\b(?:available|found)\s+at:\s*$` cleanup for dangling "available at:" lines.
