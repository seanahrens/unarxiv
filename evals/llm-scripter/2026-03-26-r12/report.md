## Paper 2: AgentProcessBench (2603.14465)

**Overall: 0.88**

### Goal 1 — Near-Verbatim Fidelity: 0.70

**Critical Issue: Systematic dropout of "reference annotations" and "reference models"**

The phrase "reference annotations" is dropped in 3 separate places throughout the script, and "reference models" is dropped in 1 place. This is a consistent pattern tied to the instruction warning "NEVER convert \\ref{label} into the word 'reference'" — the LLM is over-applying this instruction and suppressing the English word "reference" even when it's used as a plain noun.

Evidence:

1. **Source** (method.tex:132): `"auxiliary references, including official solutions and reference annotations generated by three state-of-the-art LLMs: DeepSeek-V3.2, GPT-5.2, and Claude 4.5 Sonnet"`
   **Transcript** (line 101): `"auxiliary references, including official solutions and generated by three state-of-the-art LLMs"`
   → "reference annotations" dropped

2. **Source** (method.tex:135): `"the agreement between the final human annotations and the three reference models ranges only from 66.9% to 72.1%"`
   **Transcript** (line 103): `"the agreement between the final human annotations and the three ranges only from 66.9% to 72.1%"`
   → "reference models" dropped

3. **Source** (experiment.tex:119): `"If neither prediction nor reference contains a -1 label, the trajectory is considered error-free"`
   **Transcript** (line 141): `"If neither the prediction nor the a negative one label, the trajectory is considered error-free"`
   → Garbled — "reference contains a" becomes "the a" (incoherent)

4. **Source** (appendix.tex:13): `"step-wise reference annotations from three powerful models"`
   **Transcript** (line 210): `"step-wise from three powerful models"`
   → "reference annotations" dropped

5. **Minor**: "A dashed blue diagonal  runs from the lower-left" (double space, word dropped — source had no explicit description, likely a figure inference gap)

This is the same root cause across all 4 instances: the LLM sees "reference" in prose and incorrectly associates it with the citation-stripping instruction. The instruction says "NEVER convert \\ref{label} into the word 'reference'" — the LLM interprets this as "avoid the word 'reference' generally."

**Root cause:** The `\ref{}` artifact-prevention instruction is too close in phrasing to legitimate English use of the word "reference." The model over-generalizes the prohibition.

## Root Cause Analysis

The prompt at lines 112-113 states:
```
CRITICAL: NEVER convert \\ref{label} into the word "reference" followed by the label
name — that is an artifact.
```

The model is interpreting this as "avoid the word 'reference' in output." This is technically correct for `\ref{}` artifacts (don't produce "reference sec:model") but incorrectly suppresses legitimate English usage of "reference" as a noun (e.g., "reference annotations", "reference models").

**Fix needed:** Clarify that only `\ref{}` LaTeX commands should be handled this way — the plain English word "reference" in prose must be preserved.

---

## Recommended Fix

Add clarification after the existing `\ref{}` instruction:

> **Clarification:** The plain English word "reference" used as a noun or adjective in prose (e.g., "reference model", "reference annotation", "reference point", "ground-truth reference") must be **preserved verbatim**. Only the LaTeX command `\ref{label}` should be deleted — not the word "reference" itself.
